%Begin Question---------------------------------
The bonding in GaN is predominantly what character? Note: $X_{\text{Ga}} = 1.81 $ and $ X_{\text{N}} = 3.04$:
\begin{choices}
	\correctchoice Covalent
	\choice Ionic
	\choice Metallic
	\choice Van der Waals
\end{choices}

%Solution------------------------------------------
\begin{solution}

The primary bond type in GaN is covalent. These two atomic species are not metallic, so we can exclude metallic bonding as a predominant type. As a rule of thumb, a $\Delta X$ of 1.7 yields a bond with predominantly ionic character. Or, the full calculation from the Pauling approximation for percent ionic character(\%IC): \%IC $= 100(1-exp{-\frac{1}{4}(X_{N}-X_{Ga})^2})$, where $X$ indicates the electronegativity on the Pauling scale, gives \%IC\textsubscript{GaN} = 31.5\%.  

\end{solution}
