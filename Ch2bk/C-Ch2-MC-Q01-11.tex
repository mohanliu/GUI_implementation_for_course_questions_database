%Begin Question----------------------------------
Five materials are listed below. Which do you expect to have the largest covalent bond character?
\begin{choices}
	\correctchoice Elemental silicon (Si)
	\choice LiF
	\choice Molybdenum (BCC structure)
	\choice InP
	\choice Helium gas
\end{choices}

%Solution------------------------------------------
\begin{solution}

Silicon will have the highest covalent bond character --- there is no difference between electronegativities of Si and Si and so it will share its electrons in order to achieve a stable bond configuration. LiF is an ionic solid (large $\Delta X$), Mo is a metal. InP will have a large degree of covalent bonding, but there is still some electronegativity difference $\Delta X \approx 0.4$. Helium gas only makes secondary, induced dipole bonds.

\end{solution}
