%Begin Question----------------------------------------------------
You are selecting a material that requires low thermal conductivity ($k$) and high Young's modulus ($E$) according to the materials index: $M = \frac{E^{\frac{1}{2}}}{k}$

Draw \emph{one contour line} corresponding to this materials index on Fig. \ref{fig:StiffInsulate}.
%

\begin{figure}[H]%
	\centering
	\includegraphics[width=\columnwidth]{StiffInsulating}%
	\caption{An Ashby plot of $E$ vs $k$}%
	\label{fig:StiffInsulate}%
\end{figure}


\begin{solution}

You can approach this one of two ways. The easier one is to recognize that the $k$ value is on the $y$-axis and the $E$ value is on the $x$-axis. So, we can put it in the form $y^a/x^b$. To put the materials index in this form we represent it as $k^{-1}/E^{-1/2}$ (remember to observe what is plotted on each axis). Therefore the slope on the log-log plot is $b/a = \frac{-1/2}{-1} = 1/2$.

Alternatively, you can just work it out:
%
\begin{align*}
	M &= \frac{E^{1/2}}{k}\\
	\text{log}(M) &= \frac{1}{2}\text{log}(E)-\text{log}(k)\\
	\text{log}(k) &= \frac{1}{2}\text{log}(E)-\text{log}(M)\\	
	\intertext{which is in the form $log(Y) = m\,log(X)+b$, with $m = 1/2$. The drawing for this slope is shown in the solution figure.}
\end{align*} 
%
\includegraphics[width=\columnwidth]{Ashby-MC-Q2-2_Sol}

\end{solution}
