%Begin Question----------------------------------------------------
\textbf{Sketch:} You are selecting a window material based on its refractive index ($n$) and yield strength ($\sigma_y$). You will select you material according to the materials index: $M = \sigma_y n^{2}$

Draw \emph{one contour line} corresponding to this materials index on Fig. \ref{fig:Ashby-S-Q2-3}.
%

\begin{figure}[H]%
	\centering
	\includegraphics[width=\columnwidth]{Ashby-S-Q2-3}%
	\caption{An Ashby plot of $\sigma_y$ vs $n$}%
	\label{fig:Ashby-S-Q2-3}%
\end{figure}


\begin{solution}

You can approach this one of two ways. The easier one is to recognize that the $n$ value is on the $y$-axis and the $\sigma_y$ value is on the $x$-axis. So, we can put it in the form $y^a/x^b$. 

To put the materials index in this form we represent it as $\sigma_y n^{2}$ (remember to observe what variable is plotted on each axis). The slope on the log-log plot is $b/a = -\frac{1}{2}$.

The graph below is a bit (unintentionally) tricky because of the gridding at $n = 2$ and $n = 5$. \emph{Make sure you pay attention to the numbers on the axes, not just the grid lines.}

Alternatively, you can just work it out:
%
\begin{align*}
	M &= \sigma^{1} n^{2}\\
	\mathrm{log}(M) &= \mathrm{log}(\sigma_y)+2\mathrm{log}(n)\\
	\mathrm{log}(n) &= -\frac{1}{2}\mathrm{log}(E)+\frac{1}{2}\mathrm{log}(M)\\	
	%\intertext{which is in the form $\mathrm{log}(Y) = m\,\mathrm{log}(X)+b$, with $m = -1/2$. The drawing for this slope is shown in the solution figure.}
\end{align*} 
%
\includegraphics[width=\columnwidth]{Ashby-S-Q2-3_Sol}

\end{solution}
