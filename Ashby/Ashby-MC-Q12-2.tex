%Begin question--------------------------------------------
Consider the materials selection diagram in Fig. \ref{fig:Ashby-MC-Q12-1}. In which quadrant (delineated by dashed lines and labeled on the plot) are the best materials located if you are \emph{minimizing} the materials index $M = E^3 k^2$?

\begin{choices}
\choice Quadrant 1
\choice Quadrant 2
\correctchoice Quadrant 3
\choice Quadrant 4
\end{choices}

%Solution----------------------------------
\begin{solution}

If we are minimizing $M = E^3 k^2$ we want small values of $E$ and small values of $k$. This is Quadrant 3.

You could do the entire problem, which would give you a slope of -3/2. Minimizing this value would only give you materials in in Quadrant 3 with the small exception of that one mystery material in Quadrant 2 (stone).

\end{solution}
