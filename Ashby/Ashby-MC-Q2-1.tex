%Begin Question----------------------------------------------------
In the Figure below, which plots tensile strength ($\sigma_{\text{TS}}$) vs. density ($\rho$), there are 7 lines drawn (1-7). Which lines represents a materials index of $M_i = \frac{\sigma_{\text{TS}}^{2/3}}{\rho}$? 
%
\begin{multicols}{3}
	\begin{choices}
		\choice 1
		\choice 2
		\correctchoice 3
		\choice 4
		\choice 5
		\choice 6
		\choice 7
	\end{choices}
\end{multicols}

\begin{solution}

So, you can either recognize that if you have a general materials index: $M_i = \frac{A^n}{B^p}$, and $p = 1$, you can read the slope directly from the denominator. It will be $n^{-1}$. In our case, $n = 2/3$, so the slope, $m = 3/2$. That is slope \#3.

Alternatively, you can work it out:
%
\begin{align*}
	M_i &= \frac{\sigma_{\text{TS}}^{2/3}}{\rho}\\
	log(M_i) &= \frac{2}{3}log(\sigma_{\text{TS}})-log(\rho)\\
	log(\sigma_{\text{TS}}) &= \frac{3}{2}log(\rho)+\frac{3}{2}log(M_i)
	\intertext{which is in the form $log(Y) = m\,log(X)+b$, with $m = 3/2$. The drawing for this slope is shown in the solution figure.}
\end{align*} 
%
\end{solution}
