%Begin question--------------------------------------------
The automotive industry typically works to increase fuel efficiency by decreasing vehicle weight. Accordingly, they need materials that are strong (measured with yield strength, $\sigma_y$), lightweight (measured with density, $\rho$), and cost effective (measured in cost, $C$).

Which of the following materials selection indices might you \emph{maximize} to select the optimal material?

\begin{choices}
	\choice $\frac{\sigma_y C}{\rho}$
	\choice $\frac{\sigma_y \rho}{C}$
	\correctchoice $\frac{\sigma_y}{C \rho}$
	\choice $\sigma_y \rho C$
\end{choices}

%Solution----------------------------------
\begin{solution}

We want large $\sigma_y$, small $\rho$, and small $C$. That means $\frac{\sigma_y}{C \rho}$ is our choice!

\end{solution}
