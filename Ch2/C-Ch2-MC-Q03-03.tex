%Begin Question---------------------------------
The valence of sulfur (S) is:
\begin{choices}
	\choice 1
	\choice 4
	\choice 3
	\correctchoice 6
	\choice 15
\end{choices}

%Solution-------------------------------------------
\begin{solution}

Valence is the number of electrons in the outermost electron shell. The electronic configuration for aluminum is $1s^{2}2s^{2}2p^{6}3s^{2}3p^{4}$. The $n = 1$ and $n = 2$ shells are full, and there are 6 electrons in the $n = 3$ shell, therefore the valence is 6. 
	
\end{solution}
